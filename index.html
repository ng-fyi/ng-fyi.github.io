Nanoscape

2021

Lead Artist

An educational first-person exploration game developed by the UNSW 3DVAL. The player navigates a cancer cell as a scientist shrunk to a height of 40 nanometers.





Art Direction and Environment Design

Clockwise: The development of Nanoscape's art style over the course of its development




Shaders

The surface of the cell is a seething membrane of molecules. To depict it, I built a shader using layered voronoi noise to generate an endless surface of spinning nodes. I used parallax UV mapping to create the illusion of depth, and shifting displacement patterns to imitate the undulations of the cell.




Tools

These twisting organic cables were created using a procedural spline-based tool I built in Houdini. It featured automatic UV mapping and collision testing, ensuring the bundles could be easily modelled around other geometry.
