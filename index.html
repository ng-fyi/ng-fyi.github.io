Nanoscape

2021

Lead Artist

An educational first-person exploration game developed by the UNSW 3DVAL. The player navigates a cancer cell as a scientist shrunk to a height of 40 nanometers.

Although my title was Lead Artist, I performed a number of roles on the project.

Art Direction

I guided the character and atmosphere of the experience throughout its development.

From reference through to concept art, renders and repeated iterations, I worked to create a cohesive visual language that would express the chaos and complexity of the molecular world.

Art Direction

The surface of the cell is populated by innumerable types of molecule, dozens of which are represented in Nanoscape.

In pre-rendered biomedical animation, every atom of these bodies is individually animated. I worked with the lab's specialist artists to develop an original style that was simple enough to be rendered with low-poly meshes and AO maps, but was still grounded in scientific reality.

Tech Art

The surface of the cell is a seething, swirling membrane known as the Phospholipid Bilayer. The question of how to represent this key characteristic of the environment was a major challenge from the project's beginning, and we trialled solutions from ECS spawning to vast particle effects.

Using Shader Graph, I built a complex shader that uses stacked voronoi noise patterns with parallax UV mapping to create an endless surface of spinning nodes. It uses vertex displacement and shifting fresnel patterns to imitate the undulations of the cell, and triplanar projection to apply seamlessly across separate meshes. It also fades into a scrolling texture at a distance to avoid generating moire patterns.

Production

I also coordinated the team, which included remote members. As well as day-to-day scheduling and delegation, it was my job to establish, communicate and maintain a complex animation pipeline. Prioritisation of which elements of the environment would be represented and how was a hotly debated subject amongst the lab's scientific staff, and I took responsibility for mediating these discussions and crystallising their outcomes.

Syngenta Phase 1

2021

Solo Developer

An educational iOS app introducing Syngenta's clients to a new seedcare product.

Given a broad brief and short development window. I pitched the idea of user-controlled timelapse animation to the client verbally, accompanied by a user journey document illustrated with photoshop collage. This app acted as a prototype for a larger project, which was greenlit and is being developed this year.

Syngenta Phase 2

2022

In the next phase of this project, the original design will be adapted to VR. It will take place in an immersive environment, with more complex interactions.

In preparation for departing the lab, I created extensive documentation to guide further development. This included detailed summaries of scientific context, retrospective analysis of phase 1, design documents, and concept art.

Drive Safe, Honey!

2016

3D Artist, Designer

One of my first unity projects. "Drive Safe, Honey!", or, "Mid-Life Mario Kart", is a one-map multiplayer racing game.

Developed by myself and a partner, we taught ourselves Unity by attemping to copy Mario Kart from first principles. I designed and created the track using a parametric modelling tool built in Grasshopper, and honestly I still think it's pretty good.
