Nanoscape

2021

Lead Artist

An educational first-person exploration game developed by the UNSW 3DVAL. The player navigates a cancer cell as a scientist shrunk to a height of 40 nanometers.

As Lead Artist, I fulfilled a number of roles on the project.

Shaders

The surface of the cell is a seething membrane called Phospholipid Bilayer. To depict it, I built a shader using layered voronoi patterns and parallax UVs to generate an endless surface of spinning nodes. Vertex displacement and shifting fresnel patterns imitate the undulations of the cell, and it fades into a scrolling texture at distance to avoid moire.

This shader uses animated parallax mapping to create the illusion of transparency on distant objects.

Tools

The technical centrepiece of Nanoscape was a vast simulation of cell dynamics, pre-baked in Houdini. I designed and documented the pipeline by which this data was integrated with art assets and imported to Unity.

These bundles of organic cables were created with a procedural tool I developed in Houdini. It featured optimisation controls, automatic UV mapping, and collision detection for tight integration with existing geometry.
